  • Particulate matter (PM) is everything in the air that is not a gas, some of which can be toxic. Particles which are small enough to cross the lung/blood threshold may enter the bloodstream and be transported around the body, lodging in the heart, brain and other organs. Exposure to PM can therefore result in serious impacts to health. PM2.5 refers to particles which are less than 2.5 micrometres in diameter (a human hair is typically around 12 times thicker than that). In cities, PM2.5 most often derives from vehicle tailpipe emissions and tyre and brake pad wear.
  • Safe: refers to levels which are beneath the WHO threshold of 5µg/m3.
  • Unsafe: refers to levels which are above the WHO threshold of 5µg/m3 but beneath the UK threshold of 20µg/m3
